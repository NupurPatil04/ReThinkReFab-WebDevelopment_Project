SELF-WATERING PLANTS

Uses

  • Organization: Provides a neat and tidy way to incorporate greenery, reducing clutter.
  • Space-saving: Maximizes available space by allowing plants to thrive in compact areas.
  • Low-maintenance: Requires less frequent watering, suitable for busy individuals
PROCEDURE

Required Material

  • Plastic Bottles
  • Soil
  • Seeds or Plants
  • Water
  • Sunlight
  • Monitoring Tools (such as moisture meter)
  • Pots or Containers
  • Labels
  • Gloves
  • Trowel
  • Fertilizer
  • Gardening Gloves

STEPS TO FOLLOW

  1. Cut the Bottle: Cut the plastic bottle in half horizontally. The bottom half will serve as the water reservoir, while the top half will hold the soil and plants.
  2. Create Wick: Create a wick by cutting a strip of absorbent material, such as cotton or felt. Place one end of the wick into the soil of the top half of the bottle, and let the other end hang down into the water reservoir.
  3. Add Soil and Plants: Fill the top half of the bottle with potting soil, leaving enough space for planting your seeds or plants. Plant your seeds or place your plants into the soil.
  4. Add Water: Pour water into the bottom half of the bottle, filling it up to the level of the wick. The wick will absorb water from the reservoir and deliver it to the soil as needed, keeping it consistently moist.
  5. Place in Sunlight: Position the self-watering plant in a location that receives adequate sunlight for plant growth.
  6. Monitor and Refill: Regularly check the water level in the reservoir and refill it as needed to ensure the soil remains adequately moist for the plants.
