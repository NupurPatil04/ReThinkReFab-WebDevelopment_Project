Tomato Cage

Uses

  • Building your own tomato cages offers a host of benefits, making it a worthwhile endeavor for any gardener.
  • Tomatoes are delightful plants to grow, but their vigorous growth often overwhelms the stakes or cages meant to support them.
  • If you’ve struggled with controlling the wild growth of your tomato plants, making a sturdy, reliable tomato cage could be the solution you’ve been looking for. This guide will show you how to make the ultimate tomato cage that’s not only effective but also inexpensive and easy to construct.
PROCEDURE

Required Material

  • Livestock Panel: This is a large, wire grid panel used in farming to contain livestock. You’ll find these at any hardware or farm supply store. Opt for one with openings that are 6 inches apart for ease of use.
  • Bolt Cutters: These are required to cut the livestock panel to size. They’re available at any hardware store.
  • Gloves to protect your hands while working with metal.
  • Cable ties or garden twine for securing the structure.

STEPS TO FOLLOW

  • Step 1: Count across the width of your livestock panel to six squares and use your bolt cutters to trim off the excess.
  • Step 2:Identify the length of your panel and count nine squares from one end. Cut across, just above the bottom horizontal piece of this ninth square. Do the same from the opposite end.
  • Step 3:For each panel, cut away the horizontal wires between the vertical ones at the first row up from the bottom.
  • Step 4:Place each section of the panel under a straight piece of lumber, exposing three squares against the wood’s edge. Bend the panel towards you to form a 90-degree angle.
  • Step 5:Invert the panels so the spikes are facing upwards and straighten any bent wires.
  • Step 6:Position each bent panel around your tomato plant in the garden bed, ensuring when both are installed, they form a square cage with the plant in the center.
  • Step 7:Use small cable ties to secure the two panels together. This keeps your cage tidy and stable.
